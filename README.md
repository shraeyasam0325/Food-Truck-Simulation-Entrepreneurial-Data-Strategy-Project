# Food-Truck-Simulation-Entrepreneurial-Data-Strategy-Project
I helped design and test a simulated frozen treat business using rapid prototyping and data analysis to guide strategic decisions. The project explored startup planning alongside an interactive food truck simulation, allowing us to test various business models across products, locations, and vehicle types in a dynamic, data-rich environment.

## Project Overview

This project analyzes entrepreneurial decision-making through a business simulation that evaluated multiple market strategies for a mobile frozen dessert business. The objective was to maximize revenue by continuously adapting operational decisions using customer demographics, market research, location analytics, and revenue performance.

Rather than relying on assumptions, strategic decisions were validated through iterative experimentation and quantitative analysis.

---

## Business Problem

Launching a new food business requires balancing numerous variables simultaneously, including:

- Product selection
- Customer demographics
- Competitive landscape
- Supplier relationships
- Operating location
- Resource allocation
- Growth strategy
- Profitability

The challenge was determining which combination of business decisions produced the highest financial performance while remaining scalable and sustainable.

---

## Methodology

The project followed an iterative business experimentation framework.

### Phase 1
- Developed an entrepreneurial business model
- Identified customer segments
- Conducted competitive analysis
- Evaluated supplier strategy
- Designed operational model

### Phase 2
Ran multiple business simulations while changing:

- Product offerings
- Sales locations
- Vehicle type
- Customer targeting

Each iteration produced new performance metrics that informed future decisions.

## Key Findings

The simulations demonstrated that:

- Data-driven experimentation consistently outperformed assumption-based planning.
- Customer demographics significantly influenced product demand.
- Location selection had the largest impact on daily revenue.
- Rapid iteration reduced business risk and improved decision quality.
- Continuous testing allowed the business model to evolve toward higher profitability.

## Key Data Analyst Contributions
-Conducted comparative analysis of customer demographics, product preferences (frozen yogurt vs. ice cream), and geographic foot traffic to identify high-revenue combinations.
-Leveraged weekly performance metrics to optimize location, menu offerings, and operations, achieving a 100%+ increase in revenue by Simulation B through data-driven pivots.
-Applied principles of effective prototyping, transforming early-stage assumptions into actionable strategies by analyzing real-time feedback from simulated market environments.
-Interpreted trends across multiple business variables to evaluate trade-offs in speed vs. quality, growth vs. profitability, and competition vs. collaboration.
-Synthesized qualitative insights from an entrepreneur interview into the analysis to understand real-world decision-making and risk management.

This project demonstrated how analytical thinking, data interpretation, and iterative testing can drive agile business development—even in uncertain, fast-moving markets.
---

## Results

The project successfully demonstrated how iterative experimentation and evidence-based decision-making improve business performance.

Rather than optimizing one variable independently, the analysis showed that the interaction between location, customer demographics, product offerings, and competitive conditions produced the greatest improvements in revenue and operational efficiency.
